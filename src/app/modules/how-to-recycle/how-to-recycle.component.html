Instruction on "How to Recycle"

GLASS

Collection

Collect empty glass bottles and containers at home.

Separation

Differentiate between clear, green, and brown glass. Place them in separate recycling bins if possible.

Rinsing

Rinse the glass items to remove any residue or food particles.

Labels

Remove labels, if possible, to ensure clean recycling.

METAL

Collection

Collect aluminum cans, steel cans, and other metal items (like small appliances) at home.

Cleanliness

Make sure metal items are free from non-metal materials, like plastic, rubber, or wood.

Compression

Flatten or compact metal cans to save space in your recycling bin.

OIL

Storage

If you change your own oil, store used oil in a leak-proof container. Do not mix it with other substances.

Filtration

Filtering used oil to remove impurities is not usually a home-based task, but it can extend the life of the oil.

TEXTILE

Collection

Set aside clothing and textiles you no longer need. Avoid discarding them with your regular trash.

Quality Control

Ensure that textiles are clean and free from contaminants. Damaged or stained clothing can often be recycled too.

Donation or Recycling

Consider donating gently used clothing to local charities, or find textile recycling drop-off locations in your area.
